Negative investor sentiment takes toll on bourse

The CSE yesterday kicked off on a positive note but later investor sentiment began to decline owing to month- end profit- takings.Amid those developments both indices indicated mixed reactions. The All Share Price Index went down by 34.69 points while the S and P SL20 rose by 14.84 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 5.6 billion with 12 crossings. Top seven crossings reported during the day were: Aitken Spence five million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 815 million; its shares traded at Rs 163, Laugfs Gas 6.1 million shares crossed for Rs 406 million; its shares traded at Rs 67, Haylays one million shares crossed for Rs 194.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 194.50.

LBL Energy Fund six million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 69 million; its shares traded at Rs 11.50, Chevron Lubricants 377,000 shares crossed for Rs 69 million, its shares traded at Rs 182.50, DFCC 425,000 crossed for Rs 58.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 15 and Brown’s Investments 200,000 shares crossed Rs 34.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 172.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 187 million (8.9 million shares traded), Laugfs Gas Rs 147 million (2.2 million shares traded), Chevron Lubricants Rs 122.6 million (670,000 shares traded), JAT Holdings Rs 113 million (2.1 million shares traded), Alumax Rs 99 million (4.97 million shares traded), LBL Energy Fund Rs 96.7 million (eight million shares traded) and Central Finance Rs 93.4 million (300,000 shares traded). During the day 154.8 million share volumes changed hands in 32796. Transactions.

It is said that the market showed mixed reactions by sectors, especially renewable energy, manufacturing and services.

Yesterday the rupee opened at Rs 304.35/50 to the US dollar in the spot market, more or less flat from Rs 304.35/50, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.

The rupee has fallen to 302.55/60 from the beginning of the month.

Sri Lanka stocks close up after Fitch confirms rating

ECONOMYNEXT – The Colombo Stock Exchange closed up on Thursday nearing the 22,000 mark, after Fitch Ratings confirmed Sri Lanka’s CCC+ sovereign rating saying external finances are stable.

The All Share Price Index closed up 0.46 percent, or 100.49 points at 21,951.79; while the S&P SL20 closed up 0.15 percent, or 9.42 points, at 6,148.21.

Market turnover was 6.51 billion rupees. Bank stocks contributed 1.54 billion rupees, and capital goods stocks contributed 1.35 billion rupees.

Top contributors to the ASPI were Melstacorp (up 6.75 rupees at 180.25), Access Engineering (up 3.60 rupees at 65.50), Union Bank (up 1.60 rupees at 16.90), Royal Ceramics (up 1 rupee at 46), and NDB (up 1.25 rupees at 144).

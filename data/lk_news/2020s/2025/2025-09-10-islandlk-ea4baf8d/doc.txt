British national honoured for heroic efforts in Ella bus tragedy

A heartfelt felicitation ceremony was held at the Parliament of Sri Lanka to honor Ms. Amy Victoria Gibb, a British national, for her extraordinary humanitarian service during the tragic bus accident on Ella–Ravana Ella Road on September 5, 2025.

Presided over by Deputy Minister of Tourism, Prof. Ruwan Ranasinghe, the ceremony recognized Ms. Gibb’s swift and courageous response at the scene, where she provided life-saving assistance to injured victims. Her medical expertise, calm under pressure, and deep compassion brought critical relief during a moment of national grief.

Prof. Ranasinghe expressed profound gratitude on behalf of the government and the people of Sri Lanka, commending Ms. Gibb’s selfless actions as a shining example of humanity in its purest form.

“Her bravery and kindness remind us that compassion knows no nationality,” said Prof. Ranasinghe.

The Ministry of Tourism, along with the citizens of Sri Lanka, extend their deepest appreciation to Ms. Gibb. Her service stands as a powerful testament to the strength of global solidarity and the enduring spirit of goodwill.

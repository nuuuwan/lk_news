20 freed job seekers in Myanmar arrive in Sri Lanka

ECONOMYNEXT – A group of Sri Lankans who went to Myanmar for jobs and ended up being trafficked were returned to the island this morning.

“The 20 Sri Lankans rescued from the cyber slave camp in Myanmar arrived in Sri Lanka this morning,” Minister of Foreign Affairs M U M Ali Sabry said.

The group were rescued through the efforts of the Sri Lanka Missions in Yangon and Bangkok.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs has urged the public to be cautious and to adhere to the proper procedures when pursuing employment opportunities abroad.

The Ministry said it was working to rescue the remaining 34 Sri Lankans stranded in the Myawaddy area of Myanmar, with the support of the relevant governments. (Colombo/Sep6/2024)

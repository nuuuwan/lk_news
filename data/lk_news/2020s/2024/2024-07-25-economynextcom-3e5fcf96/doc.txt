Sri Lanka rupee opens flat at 303.60/65 to US dollar, bonds down

ECONOMYNEXT – Sri Lanka’s rupee was trading almost flat at 303.60/65 to the US dollar on Thursday, dealers said. Bond yields were broadly steady, they said, and stocks opened up 0.12 percent.

The rupee closed at 303.55/65 to the greenback on Wednesday.

In the secondary market, treasury bond yields held broadly steady, despite the central bank’s policy rate cut yesterday, dealers said.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.35/43 percent, from 10.30/45 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2027 was quoted stable at 11.60/70 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 11.75/80 percent, from 11.75/85 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 12.03/10 percent, down from 12.02/12 percent.

In equities, Colombo’s All Share Price Index opened 13.97 points higher at 11,715, while the S&P SL20 of more liquid stocks opened up at 0.15 percent or 5.14 points to 3,418.

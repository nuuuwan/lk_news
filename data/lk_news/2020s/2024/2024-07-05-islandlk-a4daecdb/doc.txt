Stock Market still wary of election vibes despite ‘good news’ from external debt restructuring

The stock market kicked off on a positive stance at the outset due to the signing the agreement between the international bondholders and the Sri Lanka government on the debt restructuring exercise, but the market started declining nevertheless due to the political uncertainty arising from speculation on the upcoming Presidential elections.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. All Share Price Index down by 101.1 points while S and P SL20 down by 31.25. In the retail market the turnover stood at Rs 1.96 billion with five crossings. Those crossings were reported in JKH, which crossed 3.1 million shares to the tune of Rs 635 million and its share price traded at Rs 205, Sampath Bank 1.2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 98.5 million and its share price traded at Rs 79, HNB 370,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 73 million and its share price traded at Rs 198, E .B Creacy 1.9 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 45.6 million and its share price traded at Rs 24 and Commercial Bank 275,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 28.6 million and its share price traded at Rs 104.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were Sampath Bank Rs 265 million (3.3 million shares traded), Hayleys Rs 52 million (609,000 shares traded), NTB Rs 43.9 million (344,000 shares traded), JKH Rs 43.8 million (230,000 shares traded), Dipped Products Rs 38.5 million (1.1 million shares traded), HNB Rs 29.1 million (145,000 shares traded) and Browns Investments Rs 27.6 million (2.8 million shares traded). During the day 59.8 million share volume changed hands in 12000 transactions.

Maharaja Foods Limited yesterday opened its Initial Public Offering and all 25000 shares fully subscribed to the tune of Rs 125 million and its share price traded at Rs 5.

Meanwhile, the Central Bank yesterday announced the US dollar rate. The rupee opened at Rs 304.50/70 to the US dollar, while bond yields were broadly steady, and stocks opened 0.50 percent up, dealers said.

The rupee closed at Rs 304.20/50 to the greenback on the previous day.In the secondary market, yields were steady, dealers said.A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.75/80 percent, down 10.70/85 percent.A bond maturing on 01.05.2028 was quoted at 11.85/90 percent.A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 11.95/12.05 percent, down from 12.10/20 percent.

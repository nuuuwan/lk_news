Parliament dissolution jitters send share market into bearish sulk

The stock market yesterday was extremely bearish due to speculation on a possible dissolution of parliament and the impact it would have on the country, particularly since it would lead to policy inconsistency, stock market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 165 points, while S and P SL20 declined by 61.4 points. Turnover stood at Rs 678 million with one crossing. The crossing was reported in Hayleys, which crossed 200,000 shares to the tune of Rs 20 million; its shares traded at Rs 100.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 39.5 million (413,000 shares traded), Hayleys Rs 32.4 million (724,000 shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 31.9 million (385,000 shares traded), Browns Investments Rs 29.4 million (5.3 million shares traded), HNB Rs 20.6 million (108,000 shares traded), Royal Ceramic Rs 20.1 million (631,000 shares traded) and Commercial Bank Rs 16.24 million (159,000 shares traded). During the day 35.6 million share volumes changed hands in 9400 transactions.

Sustainable Fitch-Singapore said it has given its second-party opinion on the Commercial Bank of Sri sustainable bond framework as ‘Good’.

“We consider transactions under the sustainable bond framework to be fully aligned with the ICMA Green Bond Principles, Social Bond Principles and the Sustainability Bond Guidelines. Our Second-Party Opinion is that the alignment is ‘Good’, the opinion stated.

Yesterday the rupee opened stronger at Rs 303.15/30 to the US dollar, dealers said. Bond yields were broadly steady, they said and stocks were down 0.02 percent.

The rupee closed at Rs 303.55/65 to the greenback on Friday. In the secondary market, yields were broadly steady, dealers said. A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.45/50 percent, from 10.40/55 percent. A bond maturing on 15.12.2027 was quoted at 11.55/70 percent, up from 11.50/65 percent. A bond maturing on 01.05.2028 was quoted at 11.75/80 percent, from 11.70/85 percent. A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 12.00/15 percent, up from 12.00/10 percent.

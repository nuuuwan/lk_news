Clarification of Wealth Tax matters gives bourse a boost

The stock market got off on a positive trend yesterday but later turned negative when President Ranil Wickremesinghe mentioned a new wealth tax, which was wrongly grasped by investors.

But later the market showed some recovery due to certain tax specialists highlighting that only 10 percent of the households are to be taxed, market analysts said. Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 16.82 points while S and P SL20 index declined by 9 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 2.7 billion with nine crossings. Those crossings were reported in Ambeon Holdings, where 93.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 1.28 billion; its shares traded at Rs 13.70, Access Engineering 7.2 million shares crossed for Rs 176 million; its shares traded at Rs 24.50, Melstacope 1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 91.3 million; its shares sold at Rs 87, Hayleys 457,000 shares crossed for Rs 46 million; its shares traded at Rs 103, JKH 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 40.6 million and its shares traded at Rs 203, Richard Pieris 2 million shares crossed for Rs 40 million and its shares sold at Rs 20, Distilleries 1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 27 million; its shares sold at Rs 27, NTB 170 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 22 million; its shares fetched Rs 132.70 and HNB 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 20 million; its shares traded at Rs 200.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Dipped Products Rs 62.5 million (1.6 million shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 59.4 million (2.4 million shares traded), Hayleys Fabrics Rs 52.9 million (1.17 million shares traded), Hayleys Rs 46 million (446,000 shares traded), LOLC Holdings Rs 45 million (950,000 shares traded), NTB Rs 42 million (317,000 shares traded) and hZenid Business Solutions Rs 41.8 million (3.2 million shares traded). During the day 150 million share volumes changed hands in 11000 transactions.

hSenid Business Solutions will reallocate Rs 350 million raised through an IPO for acquisitions in APAC and East African regions, for market development.

Subject to obtaining the approval of shareholders by special resolution, “the Board of Directors proposes to utilize such sum for suitable market development activities during the current financial year ending March 31, 2025, hSenid told CSE sounces. The money was originally allocated for ‘strategic acquisitions in APAC and East African regions’.

Hela Apparel Holdings PLC is looking to raise 1.5 billion rupees through a rights issue to settle a sub-subsidiary’s debt.

Hela will issue 319,365,227 ordinary voting shares at a proportion of 8 new for every 33 existing at Rs. 5.00 each.

“To be utilised for the purpose of making funds available to the sub-subsidiary of the Company to enable such entity to settle existing bank borrowings,” the company said.

Yesterday the rupee opened weaker at 305.00/50 to the US dollar. The rupee closed at Rs 304.75/305.40 to the greenback, while bond yields were up.

In the secondary market, yields were up, dealers said. A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.25/45, up from 10.10/30 percent. The indicative figure for a bond maturing on 15.09.2027 was 10.60/80 percent, up from 10.57/60 percent. The indicative figure for a bond maturing on 01.07.2028 was 11.15/40 percent, up from 11.15/65 percent. A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 11.95/12.05 percent, up from 11.80/90.

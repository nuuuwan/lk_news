Sri Lanka rupee opens weaker at 303.60/75 to US dollar

ECONOMYNEXT – Sri Lanka’s rupee opened slightly weaker at 303.60/75 to the US dollar on Wednesday, while bond yields were broadly steady, and stocks opened 0.41 percent up, dealers said.

The rupee closed at 303.90/304.50 to the greenback on Tuesday.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.75/85 percent, up from 10.70/85 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 11.85/95 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted stable at 12.05/10 percent.

In equities, Colombo’s All Share Price Index opened 47.76 points higher at 11,832, while the S&P SL20 of more liquid stocks opened up at 0.70 percent or 24.02 points to 3,461.

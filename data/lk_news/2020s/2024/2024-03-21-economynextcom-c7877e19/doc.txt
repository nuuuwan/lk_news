Sri Lanka stocks close down, selling pressure continues

ECONOMYNEXT – The Colombo Stock Exchange closed down on Thursday, data on its site showed.

The broader All Share Index closed down 0.57 percent, or 64.40 points, at 11,176; while the S&P SL20 Index closed down 0.82 percent, or 26.25 points, at 3,182.

“The downward trend is continuing, this could possibly be because of profit-taking,” market participants said.

“Banks which were held for long, and corporates tight for cash are selling down.”

Sampath Bank Plc was down at 77.60, Hatton National Bank Plc down at 162.00, and Commercial Bank of Ceylon Plc down at 93.80.

But retail activity was prevalent even though it is lower than the last few weeks, they said. “Dividend paying counters saw retail investor interest.”

Major contributors to the Index included Ceylon Tobacco Company Plc (up at 1,223.00), Dialog Axiata Plc (up at 11.80), and SMB Finance Plc (up at 0.70). (Colombo/Mar21/2024)

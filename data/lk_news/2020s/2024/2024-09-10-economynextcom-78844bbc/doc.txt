Sri Lanka to borrow US$300mn for budget support from ADB

ECONOMYNEXT – Sri Lanka’s cabinet has approved borrowing 300 million dollars from the Asian Development Bank in programs linked to reforms in financial and power sectors, Minister Bandula Gunawardana said.

A tranche of 200 million dollars are to be given after 12 prior actions are completed. Most have been completed and the rest are in the process of being completed.

The loan is part of a 400 million dollar financial sector reform project.

Cabinet also approved borrowing 100 million from ADB under water sector reform program, which is part of 200 million dollar program. (Colombo/Aug10/2024)

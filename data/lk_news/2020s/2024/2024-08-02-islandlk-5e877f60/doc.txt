Share market on recovery track following disclosure of presidential poll survey results

The stock market yesterday bounced back after a prolonged decline due to macro- economic factors. However, it turned into a more market oriented stance with the surfacing of presidential election opinion survey results, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All- Share Price Index went up by 33.14 points while the S and P SL20 rose by 20.25 points. Turnover stood at Rs 756 million with two crossings. Those crossings were reported in TJ Lanka, which crossed 1.2 million shares to the turnover of Rs 51.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 40.50 and Sampath Bank 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 37.75 million; its shares traded at Rs 75.50.

In the retail market top companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 164 million (944,000 shares traded), Hayleys Fabrics Rs 82.8 million (1.7 million shares traded), TJ Lanka Rs 33.8 million (835,000 shares traded), Capital Alliance Rs 19.5 million (439,000 shares traded), Citizens Developments Business Finance Rs 17.7 million (84000 shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 17.7 million (211,000 shares traded) and Sampath Bank Rs 17.3 million (230,000 shares traded). During the day 25 million share volumes changed hands in 7400 transactions.

Overseas Realty in a filing to the CSE said the third largest shareholder, Shing Kwan Ltd., disposed of a 12.77 percent stake amounting to 158.77 million shares at Rs. 18 each. It also said Orthopaedic Clinic Ltd., purchased a 13.1 percent stake amounting to 163 million shares at the same price.

Shing Kwan Investment Company Ltd., and related party Shing KWan Investment (Singapore) Ltd., remain the single largest shareholders with a 41.43% stake in Overseas Realty.

Yesterday the rupee opened almost unchanged at Rs 302.35/50 to the US dollar, dealers said. Bond yields were broadly steady, they said, and stocks opened up 0.09 percent.

The rupee closed at Rs 302.40/50 to the greenback on the previous day. In the secondary market, Treasury bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said. A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.50/70 percent, up from 10.50/65 percent. A bond maturing on 01.05.2027 was quoted at 11.45.55 percent. A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 12.05/15 percent, up from 12.00/15 percent. A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 12.25/45 percent, up from 12.20/40 percent. A bond maturing on 15.10.2030 was quoted at 12.73/80 percent, down from 12.70/85 percent.

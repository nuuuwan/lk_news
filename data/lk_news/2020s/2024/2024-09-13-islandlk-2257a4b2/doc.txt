Iconic Trust increases stake in Sierra Cables to over 30 percent

Iconic Trust (Private) Limited, a subsidiary of Browns Investments PLC, said it had purchased 6,654,899 shares of Sierra Cables, increasing its stake in the company to 30.42 percent.With the increase in ownership in voting shares of Sierra Cables, Iconic Trust will make a mandatory offer to its remaining shareholders in terms of the Takeovers & Mergers Code in due course, informed sources said.

Sierra Cables voting shares were bought at Rs. 8 each, for a gross consideration of Rs 53,239, 192.

Amid those developments, the stock market yesterday got off to a good start but later selling pressure was recorded, especially for blue-chip counters, due to prevailing political uncertainties.

Both indices moved upwards slightly. Turnover stood at Rs 1.15 billion with three crossings. Those crossings were reported in Commercial Bank, where 2.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 167 million; its shares traded at Rs 78, HNB 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 77.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 155 and JKH 451,000 shares crossed for Rs 72.1 million; its shares sold at Rs 160.

In the retail market, companies that mainly contributed the turnover were; Commercial Bank Rs 242 million (3.1 million shares traded), HNB Rs 189 million (1.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 116 million (723,000 shares traded), Dialog Rs 16.7 million (1.9 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 15.3 million (224,000 shares traded), Dipped Products Rs 14 million (452,000 shares traded) and Hayleys Rs 12 million (136,000 shares traded. During the day 25.3 million share volumes changed hands in 6800 transactions.

It is said that during the trading period, banking sector counters, especially Commercial Bank and HNB, mainly contributed to the turnover. Further, manufacturing sector counters, mainly JKH, Dipped Products and Hayleys, were the second main contributor to the economy.

Yesterday, the buying rate of the dollar was Rs 296.41 and the selling rate Rs 305.69. The country’ s inflation rate was 0.50 percent.

LTL Holdings not to proceed with planned IPO, but market indices move upward

The planned IPO of LTL Holdings would not proceed pending clarification on new information, the CSE sources said yesterday.

“In this regard, the CSE has required LTL to provide additional information/clarifications to the CSE for review,” the statement said.

“In view of the aforesaid new information, the Board of Directors of the CSE noted that such information should have been disclosed in the prospectus issued by LTL, they said.

The stock market bounced back yesterday after experiencing a downfall during the past few trading days after the IMF announced that they were expecting to negotiate with the new government after the presidential election, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. All Share Price Index up by 115.8 points while S and P SL20 up by 62.4 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.2 billion with four crossings. Those crossings were reported in HNB, which crossed 810,000 shares to the tune of Rs 126.9 million and its share price traded at Rs 159, Richard Pieris 2.7 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 55.3 million and tis share price traded at Rs 20.50, Cable Solutions 3.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 21.7 million and its share price traded at Rs 7 and Hayleys Fabrics 470,000 shares crossed to the tune tune of Rs 21.2 million and its share price traded at Rs 45.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were HNB Rs 195.9 million (1.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 183 million (1.1 million shares traded), Hayleys Rs 70.2 million (794,000 shares traded), Vallibel One Rs 51.9 million (1.2 million shares traded), Browns Investments Rs 50.5 million (10.1 million shares traded) and Commercial Bank 45.7 million (566,000 shares traded). During the day  50.1 million   share volumes changed hands in  7500 transactions.

Yesterday the Central Bank announced the US dollar rate. The  rupee opened at Rs 300.70/95 to the US dollar , dealers said, while bond yields were up. A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 13.55/65 percent. A bond maturing on 15.06.2029 was quoted at 13.70/75 percent.

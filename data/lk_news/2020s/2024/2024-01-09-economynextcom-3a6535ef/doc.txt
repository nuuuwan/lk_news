Sri Lanka to pay doctors Rs35,000 allowance from Jan

ECONOMYNEXT – Sri Lanka’s cabinet has approved a proposal by President Ranil Wickremesinghe will start paying a 5,000 rupee pay hike to state workers from January and will also pay a 35,000 rupee allowance to doctors from the same month, a statement said.

A 10,000 rupee allowance out of a total of 20,000 was to be paid from April according to a budget proposal.

The cabinet also approved a proposal to double a Disturbance, Availability & Transport (DAT) allowance to doctors from 35,000 to 70,000 rupees.

University lecturers will also get a 25 percent increase in their study allowance from the January salary.

Sri  Lanka is set to get more taxes that originally planned in 2023, State Minister for Finance Shehan Semasinghe has said. (Colombo/Jan09/2023)

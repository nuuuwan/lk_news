Investors’ presidential or parliamentary poll first quandary checks share trading momentum

Stock market activities were not satisfactory yesterday due to investor uncertainty over whether President Ranil Wickremesinghe would be going for a presidential election or a parliamentary election by dissolving the parliament.

Accordingly, the market was a bit inactive and both indices moved downwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 63.25 points while S and P SL20 declined by 19.24 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 503 million without any crossings. In the retail market, top companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Keells Hotels Rs 56.32 million (3.5 million shares traded), CTC Rs 37 million (30000 shares traded). Dialog Axiata Rs 32.5 million (3.4 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 31.1 million (407,000 shares traded), L B Finance Rs 29.6 million (449,000 shares traded), Hayleys Rs 28.9 million (286,000 shares traded) and Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 24.5 million (584,000 shares traded). During the day 20.2 million share volumes changed hands in 5600 transactions.

Maharaja Foods Limited will go for an Initial Public Offering (IPO) today to raise Rs 125 million. The company will issue 25 million shares at Rs 5 per share. They will come under the Packaged Food and Meat sector in the stock market.

Yesterday, the rupee traded almost flat at Rs 303.60/65 to the US dollar, dealers said. Bond yields were broadly steady, they said, and stocks opened up 0.12 percent. The rupee closed at Rs 303.55/65 to the greenback the previous day.

In the secondary market, Treasury bond yields held broadly steady, despite the Central Bank’s policy rate cut, dealers said. A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.35/43 percent, from 10.30/45 percent. A bond maturing on 15.12.2027 was quoted stable at 11.60/70 percent. A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 11.75/80 percent, from 11.75/85 percent. A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 12.03/10 percent, down from 12.02/12 percent.

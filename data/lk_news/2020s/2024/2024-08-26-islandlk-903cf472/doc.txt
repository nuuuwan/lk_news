Surge in selling pressure in wake of governmental change agonies

The stock market was yesterday in an extremely bearish mode as most investors were agonizing over a possible change of government after the presidential election. Their worry was that a governmental change would negatively impact economic policies and upset the current status quo. Therefore, huge selling pressure was noted in the market, stock market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. The All- Share Price Index went down by 149.1 points while S and P SL20 declined by 58 points. Turnover stood at Rs 554 million with two crossings. Those crossings were reported in Hayleys, which crossed 500,000 shares to the tune of Rs 47 million; its shares traded at Rs 94 and Melstacope 400,000 shares crossed for Rs 32.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 81.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Dialog Axiata Rs 24.1 million (2.6 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 22.2 million (295,000 shares traded), LMF Rs 22 million (873,000 shares traded), HNB Rs 18 million (102,000 shares traded), Ceylinco Insurance Rs 17 million (69000 shares traded), JKH Rs 16 million (95000 shares traded) and Maharaja Food Products Rs 16.6 million (3.3 million shares traded).

During the day 31.6 million share volumes changed hands in 9700 transactions. It’s said that manufacturing companies, especially Hayley’s Melstacope and financial sector companies, performed fairly well

Yesterday the rupee opened at Rs 299.75/300.50 to the US dollar, dealers said. Bond yields were down, they said, and stocks opened down 0.02 percent.

The rupee closed at Rs 299.70/300.00 to the greenback on Friday. In the secondary market, bonds were down, dealers said. Of the actively traded bonds; a bond maturing on 01.07.2028 was quoted at 12.45/55 percent, down from 12.45/60 percent. A bond maturing on 15.06.2029 was quoted at 12.70/80 percent, down from 12.80/95 percent.

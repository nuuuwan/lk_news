Announcement of presidential poll date has depressing impact on share trading

Stock market trading lacked vibrancy yesterday on account of investor sentiment declining on the presidential election date being specified, market analysts said.The main indices moved up though. The All Share Price Index went up by 7.96 points while S and P SL 20 rose by 2.32 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 575 million with three crossings. Those crossings were reported in Sanasa Development Bank, which crossed 3.7 million shares to the tune of Rs 117 million; its shares traded at Rs 31, JKH 210,000 shares crossed for Rs 49.2 million; Ceylon Land Equities 2.45 million shares crossed for Rs 20.8 million and it shares fetched Rs 8.50.

In the retail market top six companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 45.6 million (238,000 shares traded), HNB Rs 41.3 million (216,000 shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 21.9 million (231,000 shares traded), NTB Rs 21.3 million (170 million shares traded), Maharaja Food Products Rs 18.2 million (3.5 million shares traded) and ACL Cables Rs17.8 million (212,000 shares traded). During the day 31.4 million share volumes changed hands in 5445 transactions.

Yesterday the rupee traded at Rs 303.05/15 to the US dollar, dealers said. Bond yields were up, they said, and stocks opened up 0.03 percent.

The rupee closed at Rs 303.25/40 to the greenback on the previous day. In the secondary market, Treasury bond yields were up, dealers said. A bond maturing on 15.12.2026 was quoted at 10.35/45 percent, up from 10.30/45 percent. A bond maturing on 15.12.2027 was quoted at 11.65/80 percent, up from 11.60/70 percent. A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 11.85/95 percent. A bond maturing on 15.09.2029 was quoted at 12.15/20 percent, up from 12.05/10 percent.
